Our World: Arising

An Environmentalist.
A type of person we all need to be more of.
An environmentalist is a person who is interested in or studies the environment and who tries to protect it from being damaged by human activities.
I understand that all of us are tired of hearing the same thing again and again on how to prevent and stop climate change but this needs to be reinforced in our minds multiple times for a change to actually occur.
In the previous section of this webpage, I wrote about all the difficulties and problems that we are facing or ultimately going to face in coming times.
However, in this section, I am going to write on how to combat these issues.
This is our planet, we depend on Earth to live, so it is our duty to take care and nurture nature.
The United States of America pledged to reduce the amount of greenhouse gas emmisions by 50 percent by 2030.
Narendra Modi from India promised that India would increase its renewable fuel capacity to 500 gigawatts from 156 gigawatts by 2030.
However, these pledges might not be able to live up to their statements if you take the current rate of climate change into consideration.
Luckily, there is something called the Sustainable Developement Goals.
These goals provide an insight on how to save our planet from climate change and global warming.
The United Natons had created these goals, intending for every country on Earth to fulfill these goals before 2030.
An effort must be taken to accomplish these goals or it would be too late to save Earth.
Some of the Sustainable Development Goals are provided in the list below.

  1. Clean Water and Sanitation
  2. Affordable and Clean Energy
  3. Sustainable Cities and Communities
  4. Responsible Consumption and Production
  5. Climate Action

Furthermore, In the previous section I had mentioned the different types of pollution and in the list below, I have written the 3 main steps on how to combat pollution.
I am sure that you have already heard of these steps and they are also represented in the GIF above.

  1. Reuse
  2. Reduce
  3. Recycle

Instead of becoming more dependent, we need to reduce our dependence on fossil fuels and electricity right away.
Opt for eco-friendly options like solar energy and wind power.
Walk and cycle for short distances instead of driving vehicles that produce harmful gases.
Roads are clogged with vehicles that use diesel. Even one car gone can make a difference.
For longer distances, use public transport or carpool as much as possible.
Eat more Vegetables.
If you eat more vegetarian foods like legumes, beans, nuts, fruits and vegetables; it can significantly improve your enviromental impact, as animals require lots of food, land and water to be grown to end in your mouths.
Throw away less food.
The more food you waste, the more resources and effort is wasted that was put into the growing of that particular food.
And when this wasted food ends up in a landfill, it emitts methane which is a very powerful Greenhouse Gas.
Consider the amount of travelling you do.
Aeroplanes produce a large amount of Greenhouse gases when flying which increases your contribution towards climate change.
Opt towards, if you can, meeting virtually unless for a family meet up or an emergency.
If you can't reuse something, refuse it.
